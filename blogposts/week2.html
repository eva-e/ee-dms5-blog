About Me

Hello! My name is Eva and I am a pianist/instrumental composer and emerging Multimedia Artist. My journey in music began with piano at the age of 7, blooming into my most cherished instrument, to presently working with other forms of sound composition including favourite genres such as classical, electronic, synth, opera, traditional European/Asian and jazz. I have a wide range of genres that I love, however the music I tend to compose leans more towards classical, soundtrack, ambient, dreamcore and synth, in the form of instrumental pieces.

I compose on my Kawai digital upright piano with MIDI, using Ableton Live as my main DAW. I started on FL, however after taking Sound Composition and Production last year, I switched to Ableton, with it now being my preferred DAW. I don’t perceive myself as an advanced or highly skilled musician, however I humbly love to compose, create and articulate the poetic realm of music in my compositions, hoping to connect with my audience on a deeper level. Before composing I tend to write and let the words flow, constructing a short story that weaves into a poem.

I love how music and sound allows us to communicate with others on a poetic level. While we undoubtedly feel similar or distinct emotions towards sound, there is also a connectedness through the poetic connotations of sound. For example, when I hear a drum, it doesn’t just thud, it takes the form of a beating heart, a word left unsaid or yearning to be yelled out. Most of the time we don’t realise the impact that sound has on us individually and collectively. It has the ability to manipulate our sense of time as well as reveal profound emotions that were once unreachable, felt from deep within.

Currently, I am still learning and progressing in this journey of sound composition and am eager to further develop skills in sound design, score, music and game soundtrack composition, as well as furthering how I can compose poetically.

Timbre Reflection

I created a track similar to an ambient abyss, with the synth resembling time suspended in an endless atmosphere. I was experimenting with different octaves and chords, changing between single notes and chords. I was also releasing the keys rapidly and slowly to alter how the reverb would fade, which sounded at times similar to a ripple. Without the presence of a melody, the track followed an abstract experimentation with this specific sound, found on the Kontakt 8 Player, called Forest Flutes from Ethereal Earth.
